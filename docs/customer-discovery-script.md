CUSTOMER DISCOVERY SCRIPT: ROSTERSYNC ENTERPRISE TIER

=================================================================
BEFORE THE CALL
=================================================================
- Research the prospect's company (recent news, current data providers)
- Prepare to take notes -- don't filter what they say, just capture it
- Goal: 45-60 minute call, 80% listening

=================================================================
OPENING (2-3 min)
=================================================================

"Thanks for making time. I want to understand your sports data workflow
so we can build something that actually solves your problems -- not just
give you another API. This isn't a sales pitch, it's a discovery
conversation. There are no wrong answers."

=================================================================
SECTION 1: CURRENT STATE (10-15 min)
=================================================================

Q1: "Walk me through how your team currently gets sports roster data."
    - Probe: What APIs are they using? What spreadsheets or manual
      processes exist?
    - Probe: Who on the team owns this?

Q2: "What does your on-air talent use the data for on a typical
    broadcast day?"
    - Probe: How do commentators know how to pronounce names?
    - Probe: Where do graphics and stats come from?

Q3: "What data provider(s) are you working with right now?"
    - Probe: What do you like about them?
    - Probe: What do you hate about them?

Q4: "How much are you paying for sports data right now?"
    - Probe: Is it per-month, per-API call, per-seat?
    - Probe: What's included vs. extra?

=================================================================
SECTION 2: PAIN POINTS (10-15 min)
=================================================================

Q5: "What breaks most often? Where do you get burned by bad or missing
    data?"
    - Probe: Is it accuracy? Speed? Coverage?
    - Probe: What happens when it goes wrong? (live broadcast issues,
      angry talent, etc.)

Q6: "What takes the most time that you wish was automated?"
    - Probe: Manual lookups? Updating graphics systems? Correcting
      pronunciation errors?

Q7: "What would your ideal sports data provider do that no one is
    doing right now?"

Q8: "What would make you leave your current provider -- even if they're
    mostly working?"

=================================================================
SECTION 3: ENTERPRISE NEEDS (15 min)
=================================================================

Q9:  "What does 'SLA' mean to you in practice? If we guaranteed 99.9%
     uptime -- what would that actually protect you from?"
     - Probe: What happened the last time a data provider went down
       during a live broadcast?

Q10: "How important is it to be the first to know about a roster
     change -- versus being accurate but slightly delayed?"

Q11: "Do you use a DAM system (Iconik, CatDV, etc.) for your media
     assets?"
     - If yes: "What would it mean to you if roster changes
       automatically updated your DAM metadata?"
     - If no: Skip DAM questions.

Q12: "What's your budget range for a sports data solution that actually
     solves your problems?"
     - Probe: Ballpark -- $500/month? $2,000/month? $10,000/month?
     - Probe: What would you have to get to justify switching?

Q13: "If you signed with us today, what would have to be true in 6
     months for you to call it a success?"

=================================================================
SECTION 4: CLOSE (5 min)
=================================================================

Q14: "If we built exactly what you described -- accurate data, fast
     updates, phonetics for your talent, DAM sync -- what would make
     you hesitate to sign a contract today?"

Q15: "Who else should I be talking to at other networks/companies
     that have similar needs?"
